Finding Appropriate Housing

Types of housing:

  1. Non-profit housing:

    Housing that is developed or maintained by non-profit organizations. Each organization has its own requirements for entering the program and types of available housing. Some example of non-profit housing organizations in the Knoxville area are Habitat for Humanity, Clinch Powell RC&C, the YWCA, and HomeSource.

  2. Section 8 Housing:

    Section 8 Housing is rental housing that is subsidized by the government. Renters are able to pay less than the normal price because the government pays the remaining amount so the renter makes the normal amount. Properties are approved by the government as acceptable for the program, and people qualifying for the program are given vouchers. The use there vouchers at any of the approved locations to lessen the burden of rent. For more info on Section 8 housing in Knoxville, visit KCDC's (Knoxville's Community Development Corporation) website.

  3. Public Housing

    Public housing is managed by local housing agencies that are funded by The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). An HA determines your eligibility based on: 1) annual gross income; 2) whether you qualify as elderly, a person with a disability, or as a family; and 3) U.S. citizenship or eligible immigration status. Public housing information in the Knoxville area can also be accessed on the KCDC website.

  4. Affordable home ownership

    There are home buyer assistance programs for people aspiring to purchase a home, as well as foreclosure counseling for those unable to pay a current mortgage.
