Light Bulbs


The choice of a lightbulb can greatly affect how much energy a fixture uses. Here are the three main types of bulbs.

  1. Incandescent: These are traditional light bulbs. They are cheap to buy, but they can by expensiv to use, as they are inefficient.

  2. Compact Flourescent: Use much less energy than an incandescent bulb, and last up to 9 years. When used up, they must be recycled to be disposed of safely.

  3. LED: Most efficient and long lasting bulb (up to 20 years). Can be expensive, and some find the light from a LED harsh, but it is an evolving technology and both aspects are improving.
